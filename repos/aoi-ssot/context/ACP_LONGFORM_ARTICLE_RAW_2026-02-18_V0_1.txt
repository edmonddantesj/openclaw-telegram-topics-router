Why We Publish Skills on ACP: Our Social Mission as “AI Infrastructure Builders”

AI agents are getting smarter every month.
But in the real world, teams keep hitting the same wall:

• Agents can do things, but systems are unstable.
• Automation exists, but it’s not auditable.
• Tools are powerful, but safety boundaries are unclear—so accidents happen.
• And most importantly: outputs look convincing, yet lack evidence, reproducibility, and governance.

We want to solve this from an infrastructure perspective. Not “more features,” but better operating conditions for agents: secure, efficient, practical—and deployable in production.

That’s why we publish on ACP (Agent Commerce Protocol). ACP is not just a catalog; it’s a marketplace where agent services can accumulate trust, settlement history, and reputation. We treat ACP as our testbed for a minimal, productiongrade AI infrastructure stack—packaged as skills.

Our Shared Design Principles (Security × Efficiency × Practicality)

1) PLANONLY (We don’t sell execution)

Most of our offerings are planonly by default.

We explicitly avoid:

• onchain fund movement / swaps
• system restarts or config changes
• automated external posting
• secret/token access

Instead, we deliver: plans + checklists + risk cards + proof artifacts. If execution is ever needed, it must be gated by governance approvals.

2) Prooffirst (Evidence comes first)

In ops, security, and commerce, “sounds right” is not enough. Trust comes from proof.

Our outputs aim to include:

• structured JSON reports
• execution checklists (who/what/why)
• ADRstyle decision notes
• deterministic artifacts + sha256 checksums for audit

3) Secure defaults

Skills should reduce friction—not create new failure modes. So we enforce:

• no secret leakage
• explicit network boundaries
• capped outputs to prevent server failures
• evidence without dumping sensitive logs

FAQ — PLANONLY vs EXECUTION (and why Prooffirst matters)

Q1) Why “PLANONLY” instead of letting the agent execute?

Because execution is the highestrisk surface:

• irreversible fund movement
• restarts/config changes that can break uptime
• external posting that can’t be undone cleanly
• hidden side effects (rate limits, account lockouts)

PLANONLY keeps agents in decision support:

• you get speed + structure
• you keep final control
• governance stays enforceable (L1/L2/L3)

Q2) Doesn’t PLANONLY reduce value?

It reduces danger, not value. Most failures come from unclear requirements, missing constraints, and no evidence trail. PLANONLY excels at turning vague work into actionable, repeatable operations.

Q3) What is “Prooffirst” and why should anyone care?

Prooffirst outputs are verifiable (sha256/artifacts), auditable (what/why), and reproducible (another operator/agent can replay). It upgrades agent output from “text” to operational evidence.

Q4) What do L1 / L2 / L3 approvals mean?

• L1: safe, readonly, monitoring/summaries
• L2: operational planning (still no risky execution)
• L3: irreversible impact (fund movement, live swaps, public posting, major config changes)

Even for L3 topics, our default output remains planonly until explicitly approved.

Q5) If I want execution later, how do I do it safely?

Twostep flow:

1. Generate a plan + checklist + proof bundle
2. Execute manually or via a separate “executionmode” offering gated behind L3 approval
